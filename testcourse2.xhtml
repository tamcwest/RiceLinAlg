Definition. Suppose [mathjaxinline]V[/mathjaxinline] is a vector space over a field [mathjaxinline]F[/mathjaxinline]. A list of vectors [mathjaxinline]\{ v_1; v_2; \ldots v_ n\}[/mathjaxinline] in [mathjaxinline]V[/mathjaxinline] is a basis of [mathjaxinline]V[/mathjaxinline] if the list is linearly independent and spans [mathjaxinline]V[/mathjaxinline].

Definition. The list [mathjaxinline]\{ e_1; e_2; \ldots e_ n\}[/mathjaxinline] in [mathjaxinline]\mathbb R^ n[/mathjaxinline] is called the standard basis of [mathjaxinline]\mathbb R^ n[/mathjaxinline].

Let [mathjaxinline]v_1 = \left[ \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array} \right][/mathjaxinline], [mathjaxinline]v_2 = \left[ \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{array} \right][/mathjaxinline], and [mathjaxinline]v_3 = \left[ \begin{array}{c} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \end{array} \right][/mathjaxinline] be three vectors in [mathjaxinline]\mathbb R^3[/mathjaxinline].

Is the list [mathjaxinline]\{ v_1; v_2; v_3\}[/mathjaxinline] linearly independent? (You may need to do some row reduction.)

Does the list [mathjaxinline]\{ v_1; v_2; v_3\}[/mathjaxinline] span [mathjaxinline]\mathbb R^3[/mathjaxinline]?

Is the list [mathjaxinline]\{ v_1; v_2; v_3\}[/mathjaxinline] a basis of [mathjaxinline]\mathbb R^3[/mathjaxinline]?

Answer:

We can answer these questions by row reducing the matrix with columns [mathjaxinline]v_1[/mathjaxinline], [mathjaxinline]v_2[/mathjaxinline], and [mathjaxinline]v_3[/mathjaxinline]. If we do this row reduction, we obtain the [mathjaxinline]3\times 3[/mathjaxinline] identity matrix. This has a pivot in every column (i.e., no free variables), so the list [mathjaxinline]\{ v_1; v_2; v_3\}[/mathjaxinline] is linearly independent. In addition, it has a pivot in every row, so the list spans [mathjaxinline]\mathbb R^3[/mathjaxinline]. Since both conditions hold, the list is a basis of [mathjaxinline]\mathbb R^3[/mathjaxinline].

Now consider the matrix [mathjaxinline]A = \left[ \begin{array}{ccc} 1 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 & 4 \end{array}\right].[/mathjaxinline]

[mathjaxinline]A[/mathjaxinline] is a vector in the vector space of [mathjaxinline]3\times 3[/mathjaxinline] matrices [mathjaxinline]M_{3\times 3}[/mathjaxinline].

Is the list [mathjaxinline]\{ A\}[/mathjaxinline] linearly independent in [mathjaxinline]M_{3\times 3}[/mathjaxinline]?

Does [mathjaxinline]\{ A\}[/mathjaxinline] span [mathjaxinline]M_{3\times 3}[/mathjaxinline]?

Is the list [mathjaxinline]\{ A\}[/mathjaxinline] a basis of [mathjaxinline]M_{3\times 3}[/mathjaxinline]?

Answer:

A list which only has one non-zero vector in it is automatically linearly independent. However, [mathjaxinline]\{ A\}[/mathjaxinline] does not span the space [mathjaxinline]M_{3\times 3}[/mathjaxinline], since there are many matrices that cannot be expressed as [mathjaxinline]cA[/mathjaxinline] for some scalar [mathjaxinline]c[/mathjaxinline]. Hence the list is not a basis of [mathjaxinline]M_{3\times 3}[/mathjaxinline].
